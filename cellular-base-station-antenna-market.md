<p><p>The cellular base station antenna market consists of two primary types: macrocells and microcells. Macrocells are large antennas that cover wide areas, typically used for urban and rural coverage, providing long-range connectivity. In contrast, microcells are smaller antennas designed for dense areas, such as city centers or stadiums, to enhance capacity and coverage in locations with high user demand. Both types play crucial roles in ensuring efficient wireless communication by addressing different coverage and capacity requirements.</p></p>
