<p><p>The low-frequency hydrophone array market is categorized into two main types: passive sensors and active sensors. Passive sensors detect and analyze sounds from underwater sources without emitting signals, making them ideal for surveillance and environmental monitoring. Active sensors, on the other hand, emit sound waves and monitor their reflections to gather data about underwater objects and environments. Each type serves distinct applications, with passive sensors focusing on listening and data collection, while active sensors emphasize interaction and measurement of underwater phenomena.</p></p>
